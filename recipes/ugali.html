Ugali Recipe

Description

Ugali is a staple dish in many African countries, particularly in Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. It is a simple yet filling dish made from maize flour and water, forming a thick porridge-like consistency. Often served as an accompaniment to vegetables, meats, or stews, Ugali is known for its ability to complement a variety of flavors and provide essential carbohydrates for energy.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups maize flour (cornmeal)
  • cups water
  • A pinch of salt (optional)

Steps

  1. Bring the water to a boil in a medium-sized pot.
  2. Gradually add the maize flour while stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
  3. Reduce the heat and keep stirring until the mixture thickens.
  4. Continue cooking and stirring for about 5-10 minutes until the ugali is firm and pulls away from the sides of the pot.
  5. Remove from heat and shape into a mound.
  6. Serve hot with vegetables, meat, or a sauce of your choice.
